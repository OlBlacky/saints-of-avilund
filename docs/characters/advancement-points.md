# Advancement Points (AP)

AP is the universal currency for character creation and development. Every choice — attributes, skills, abilities, features — is paid for with AP.

## Budget

| Level | AP Gained | Cumulative Total |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | 100 | 100 |
| 2 | 100 | 200 |
| 3 | 100 | 300 |
| 4 | 100 | 400 |
| 5 | 100 | 500 |
| 6 | 100 | 600 |
| 7 | 100 | 700 |
| 8 | 100 | 800 |
| 9 | 100 | 900 |
| 10 | 100 | 1000 |

**Guardrail:** Spending all 1000 AP on attributes alone (getting all 6 to +5) is theoretically possible but leaves nothing for abilities, skills or features. This would be a very strange character.

---

## Attribute Costs

Each +1 to an Attribute costs more than the last. In-class attributes are cheaper.

| Rank | In-Class (incremental) | In-Class (cumulative) | Out-of-Class (incremental) | Out-of-Class (cumulative) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| +1 | 5 | 5 | 10 | 10 |
| +2 | 10 | 15 | 20 | 30 |
| +3 | 20 | 35 | 40 | 70 |
| +4 | 30 | 65 | 60 | 130 |
| +5 | 35 | 100 | 70 | 200 |

*2 in-class attributes to +5 (200 AP) + 4 out-of-class attributes to +5 (800 AP) = 1000 AP*

## Ability Costs

Characters begin with 2 Class Standard and 1 Class Special ability bundled with their first Class.

### Buying New Abilities
The economy is designed to nudge characters toward higher-tier abilities rather than stacking many lower-tier ones.

| Purchase | In-Class Cost | Note |
|---|---|---|
| 3rd Class Standard | 14 AP | Costs more than a 2nd Special — nudges toward Special |
| 4th Class Standard | 18 AP | |
| 2nd Class Special | 12 AP | Costs more than a 1st Main — nudges toward Main |
| 3rd Class Special | 22 AP | |
| 1st Class Main | 10 AP | |
| 2nd Class Main | 15 AP | |

*Out-of-class ability costs are approximately 1.5–2× in-class cost.*

### Improving Abilities
Each ability has 5 tiers; you start at Tier 1 and can improve 4 times. Improvement always costs less than buying new.

| Tier | Standard | Special | Main |
|---|---|---|---|
| → 2 | 5 AP | 4 AP | 2 AP |
| → 3 | 8 AP | 6 AP | 2 AP |
| → 4 | 12 AP | 8 AP | 3 AP |
| → 5 | 15 AP | 10 AP | 4 AP |
| **Total to max** | **40 AP** | **28 AP** | **11 AP** |
