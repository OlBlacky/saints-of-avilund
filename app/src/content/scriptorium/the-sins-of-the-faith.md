Sins of the Faith

I have actually arranged these a bit different. Almost chronologically. The first 11 were the original commandments, set down by Saint Severinius in the Rule of St Avitus. The second set of 11 were added as a positive note along the way by a Scion or by the Conclave itself. The last 11 were added hodgepodge along the years, and represent all manner of lunacy along the way. They have, no bearing, at this point, to severity. A matter for theological discussion, maybe?

Needless to say, change whatever you like.

Sins of the First Degree 1. You shall honour your family and ancestors 2. You shall not murder your brother 3. You shall not torment your brother 4. You shall not lay with your sister 5. You shall not lay with your brother's wife 6. You shall not lay with your mother 7. You shall not speak falsehoods about your brother 8. You shall not speak falsehoods to your brother 9. You shall not practice witchcraft 10. You shall stand and be judged 11. You shall worship none but the Saints

Sins of the Second Degree 1. You shall be industrious 2. You shall be kind 3. You shall be loyal 4. You shall be pious 5. You shall be truthful 6. You shall be generous 7. You shall be charitable 8. You shall be chaste 9. You shall be educated 10. You shall be cleanly 11. You shall be peaceful

Sins of the Third Degree 1. The practice of usury is to be considered a sin most foul 2. Poultry shall be like poison to you on the holy days of the Saints 3. The excesses of the body shall be like the heresy of the soul 4. The infidels must be shown the heresy of their existence 5. The pursuit of science and education is heresy most foul 6. The fire of witchcraft shall be extinguished by the fire of faith 7. Only demons ply their trade in the dark of night 8. You shall tithe the appropriate amount set by your canon 9. Injuring a clergyman carries a penalty of 50 lashes 10. Only the saintly shall be cured 11. You shall not gaze upon the Scion
