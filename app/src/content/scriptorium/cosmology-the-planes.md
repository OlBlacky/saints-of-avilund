The Prima Plana Like a beam of white light when viewed through a prism, so does the Prima Plana break down into its composite parts. These planes are one and the same as the Prima Plana, but concurently also exist separately. Like the various colours and radiations of light when separated by a prism.

At its core, the Prima Plana can be separated into two principal elements: matter and energy. Matter, or Materia Mundale, represents the entirety of the material world: plants,

animals, minerals, etc. Energy, or Energia Magica, is the force that acts upon and from that matter shaping the world. In its most basic form, it represents the ability of a creature or thing to move. In its more advanced and mysterious forms, it allows for acts of immense power through spells and enchantments.

Both matter and energy form equal parts of our world. When joined together, they create the Prima Plana Plana (the First World of Worlds) which forms all of our reality and existence.

But, like a Prism splitting a beam of white light, the Prima Plana Plana can be broken down and its component parts theoretically viewed. These are detailed in the chart above.

First, the Plana Positiva: the Elemental Plane of Positive Material and Energy. This is the World of Life: Plana Vitalis, from which we get our very ability to live.

Then the Plana Ethernale: The Ethereal Plane, a border, or transitional plane that simultaneously shields the immense power of Life from consuiming the rest of the planar spectrum, and allows this same power to be distributed.

Then their counterbalances: the Plana Negativa: the Elemental Plane of Negative Material and Energy. This is not the World of Death, but rather the World of Undeath: Plana Mortis. A complete absence of life. The Plana Sombra: the Shadow Plane, the border that keeps the Negativa in check, like the Ethereal does the Positiva.

Finally, the four base elements: Aereana, Fierana, Terrana, and Aquatica: Air, Fire, Earth and Water energy and material.

All together, these 6 major Planes and 2 border planes mix together in perfect balance to form the material and energy that in turn achieve a perfect balance and form the Prima Plana Plana: the First World of Worlds.

The Plana Celestia The Ultimate Plane, the High Plane of Existence. Celestia is a lost world, abandoned and locked. Eons ago, the Celestial beings who inhabited this place were forced to abandon it and have become exiled into the Abyss. Virtually nothing is now known of Celestia, which only furthers the belief in its glory and tranquility, and furthers the desire to reach it. What is known is that since its abandonment, all mortal souls have been condemned to the Abyss upon their deaths. For the fortunate ones who obtain the favour of a Saint in life, their soul might be led to the Celestial Kingdom in the Abyss, where they can join the legions who await the return to the Plana Celestia.

Celestials and Infernals believe that access to Celestia exists only from the Prima Plana. That is, it cannot be reached from their exile in the Abyss. If it were to be unlocked, it is assumed that they would need to find a way to leave the Abyss and enter the World before they could travel, in turn, into Celestia. It is presumed that this event, should it ever become possible, would not be a peaceful one on the Prima Plana. Plenty of apocalyptic prophesies and texts exist describing the total destruction this event will one day wreak on the world.

The Plana Abyssa The Abyss. The plane of ultimate torture and doom. This is the realm of Demons, the native creatures. But it is now also the realm of the Infernal Devils: fallen Celestials who

have become corrupted by the eons of existence in the damnation of the Abyss. It is also home to those remaining Celestials, few though they are now, who maintain their exiled Kingdom in preparation for a return to Celestia. The Abyss is composed of a myriad of warring "kingdoms" or territories within its infinity.

When a mortal soul arrives in the Abyss, following the body's death in the Prima Plana, they enter what has become known as the Valley of Darkness: the gateway to eternal damnation. Here the demons of the Abyss await, ready to feast upon the unprepared souls. But here, also, will the Saints and Celestials meet their chosen and assist the pious soul through the Valley and into their protected Kingdom. And here, also, will the Devils wait to ambush and enslave them all.

It is impossible to know what the Abyss is truly composed of, or how its political and geographic territories work: the only method of entering the Abyss is through the death of the mortal body, and the only methods for leaving the Abyss are known only to the most powerful of Abyssal beings. It is known, however, that the Valley of Darkness exists as the gateway, and that the Celestials maintain a well-fortified and hidden realm somewhere in the upper reaches of the Abyss. It is inferred that the Infernals (the corrupted fallen Celestials) have a multitude of warring realms each led by the most powerful (or cunning) devil at the time, and each engaged in a variety of plots and subterfuges aime at total conquest of the Abyss, the mortal world and eventually Celestia. It is likewise inferred that the native demons of the Abyss maintain no organisation whatsoever, but do confine themselves to specific territories: like predatory animals fighting off rivals and staking claim to a rich domain.

It is possible that creatures and beings exist in the Abyss that we have never encountered or heard of before.

Planas Oscuras There exist two more planes in our cosmology: Plana Astral and Plana Temporal. The Astral Plane forms the infinite emptiness in which the other planes exist, while the Temporal Plane forms the energy that animates and moves the entire cosmology.
