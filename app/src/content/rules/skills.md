---
title: Skills
summary: Trained areas of expertise, each tied to an attribute — how they are rolled, capped, and specialised.
---

A skill is a trained area of expertise. To use one, roll **d20 + the attribute + your skill bonus**, against a difficulty the Game Master sets. Most skills list more than one attribute — you choose **which fits your approach** *(intimidating with a raised fist is Strength; with a cold word, Charisma)*.

**Training caps.** The skills your **Class** grants — its **Class Skills** — and the two your **Path** grants — its **Additional Class Skills** — can be raised to **+2**. Any other skill you pick up caps at **+1**.

**Specialities.** A few skills are taken in a chosen field: **Craft, Profession, Perform,** and **Trade** *(e.g. Craft: Poison)*. **Religion** likewise names a faith — **Religion (Saintly Faith)**, **Religion (Black Faith)**, and others.

## The Heal skill in play

Anyone trained in **Heal** can do three things with no special ability:

- **Diagnose** — read an affliction: what poison or disease, and how grave.
- **Stabilize** — steady a dying creature: a Heal check at **DC 10 + how far below 0 HP they are**.
- **Tend Wounded** — **4 hours' care per patient, DC 11**; on a success, spend **1 Supply** from a Healer's Kit and the patient recovers **+1 HP over the day's baseline**.

The Physician and the Friar carry far stronger versions of all three. *(See [Combat](../combat/) for the dying and Wounded rules these treat.)*
