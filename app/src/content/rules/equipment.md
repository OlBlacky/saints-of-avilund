## Armour &amp; Shields

Armour raises your **Armoured Defenses** and grants **Damage Reduction (DR)**. There are four armour proficiencies — **Unarmoured, Light, Medium,** and **Heavy** — plus two shield proficiencies, **Light Shield** and **Heavy Shield**. (Unarmoured is a fighting *style* you take Feats in; there is nothing to buy.)

Your **AC** is your Armoured Constitution Defense — the number most physical attacks must beat. Armour's bonus is flat **by tier**: Light **+1**, Medium **+2**, Heavy **+3** to your Armoured Defenses. *Within* a tier, the heavier pieces buy more **DR** (and a small perk on the third) at the price of weight, coin, and drawback.

### Armour

| Armour | Tier | AC | DR | Perk | Speed | Stealth | Str | Cost | Weight |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Leather | Light | +1 | 0 | — | — | — | — | 10 sp | 15 lb |
| Studded Leather | Light | +1 | 1 | — | — | — | — | 25 sp | 20 lb |
| Hide | Light | +1 | 1 | Resist Cold 1 | — | — | — | 15 sp | 25 lb |
| Chain Shirt | Medium | +2 | 1 | — | −5' | −1 | — | 50 sp | 25 lb |
| Scale / Ring Mail | Medium | +2 | 2 | — | −5' | −1 | — | 100 sp | 30 lb |
| Breastplate | Medium | +2 | 2 | Mobility | — | −1 | — | 200 sp | 20 lb |
| Chain Mail | Heavy | +3 | 2 | — | −10' | −2 | +1 | 150 sp | 40 lb |
| Splint / Banded Mail | Heavy | +3 | 3 | — | −10' | −2 | +2 | 200 sp | 45 lb |
| Full Plate | Heavy | +3 | 3 (**4 vs Area-Effect**) | sealed vs blasts | −10' | −2 | +2 | 1500 sp | 50 lb |

- **Light** armour carries **no penalties** at all.
- **Medium** armour takes **−1 Stealth** — the **Breastplate** keeps the Stealth penalty but loses the Speed penalty (its **Mobility**).
- **Heavy** armour takes **−10' Speed and −2 Stealth**, and needs the listed **Strength**. Your proficiency advances and Armour Specialization Feats buy these drawbacks back down.

### Shields

A shield gives **both its AC and its DR only while it is raised** — nothing is passive but its weight and Speed. Each occupies a hand, so you cannot wield a two-handed weapon while shielded.

| Shield | Proficiency | AC | DR | Speed | Cost | Weight |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Buckler | Light Shield | +1 | — | — | 5 sp | 3 lb |
| Standard Shield | Light Shield | +1 | 1 | — | 12 sp | 6 lb |
| Heater / Kite / Round | Heavy Shield | +2 | 1 | −5' | 30 sp | 12 lb |
| Tower Shield | Heavy Shield | +2 | 2 | −10' | 60 sp | 25 lb |

A shield's AC stacks on your armour's. **Raising a shield** can be done two ways:

- **Anyone proficient** may raise their shield as a **Standard action**, gaining its AC and DR until the start of their next turn — re-paid every turn.
- The **Raise Shield** ability (Arms) does it far better: from a Move action down to **Free** — eventually every turn for nothing — and its Feats extend your Shield DR to the allies you protect.

## Medical &amp; Ministration Kits

A kit holds a **Supply** value of **0–20** — the consumables it spends in use. Hands-on healing and several abilities draw down Supplies; an empty kit is just the tools and the bag.

| Item | Supply | Cost | Weight |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Healer's Kit** *(empty — tools &amp; bag)* | — | 10 sp | 3 lb |
| **Friar's Kit** *(empty — oils, incense &amp; implements)* | — | 10 sp | 3 lb |
| Supplies *(per 10)* | +10 | 1 sp | 1 lb |

- The **Healer's Kit** holds bandages, salves, and thread — the physician's consumables. Its Supplies fuel hands-on medicine: a trained healer's **Tend Wounded**, and the Physician's healing abilities.
- The **Friar's Kit** is the spiritual counterpart — holy oils and incense — identical in weight, cost, and Supply mechanics, but **some ministrations require it instead of the Healer's Kit** (for instance, treating damage to the mind and soul).
- A kit filled to 20 Supplies weighs about **5 lb** and costs about **12 sp**.

## Poisons

A poison is defined by its **Application** *(ingestion, inhaled, wound, or contact)*, a **Save DC**, an **Onset**, and then — once it takes hold — a **Duration** *(how long one interval lasts)* and an **Interval count** *(how many ticks it runs)*. At each interval the victim saves again, and the poison worsens or eases. Coating a weapon by the **Wound** method takes a Minor action and a Wisdom check *(DC 10)*; one dose serves a single weapon or missile and lasts 24 hours.

Poisons are **crafted** with the **Craft (Poison)** skill — here is a sample.

## Wealth

The base coin of the realm is the **Silver Piece (sp)** — every price in this book is written in silver. But the coin in an ordinary hand is the **Copper Piece (cp)**. Copper buys a meal; silver buys a day; gold, most people never hold at all.

| Coin | Value | In practice |
|---|---|---|
| **Copper Piece (cp)** | — | The working coin. Meals, drink, a night's bed. |
| **Silver Piece (sp)** | 10 cp | A guilded labourer's daily wage. |
| **Gold Piece (gp)** | 10 sp *(100 cp)* | Rarely seen. A working man may go years without touching one. |

Coins are struck by whoever rules — a city, a republic, a bishopric, a crown — so the faces differ from polity to polity and old money still circulates alongside new. At the table it makes no difference. Copper is copper.

### A Day's Living

A young tradesman in a town or city spends his day roughly like this:

| Expense | Cost |
|---|---|
| Breakfast | 1 cp |
| Lunch | 1 cp |
| Dinner | 2 cp |
| Drink | 1 cp |
| Rented room | 5 cp |
| Guild dues | 1 cp |
| **Daily total** | **11 cp** |

That comes to **33 sp a month**. Add perhaps **10 sp** for clothes, tools, and entertainment, and he lives on about **43 sp a month** — a little over **500 sp a year**, which is 50 gold he will never see in one place.

Note which line is the largest: the **room**, at 15 sp a month, is better than a third of everything he spends. It is the single expense that decides how a person lives. Those who cannot carry it share a bed four to a room, take live-in work, eat two meals instead of three, or go home to family. Rent is the difference between the poor and the desperate.

### Wages

| Work | Per day | Per month *(25 days)* |
|---|---|---|
| Child or unskilled labour | 5 cp – 1 sp | 12–25 sp |
| Household servant *(plus board)* | 1 sp | 25 sp + keep |
| Guilded labourer | 2 sp | 50 sp |
| Scribe, or an entry professional | 3 sp | 75 sp |
| Journeyman of a guild | 4–5 sp | 100–125 sp |
| Young barrister | 5 sp | 125 sp |
| Guild master | 8–10 sp | 200–250 sp |
| Established professional | 10–20 sp | 250–500 sp |
| Clergy | nothing – 100 sp | wildly variable |

Set that against the 43 sp a month it costs to live and the shape of things is plain. **The guilded labourer clears about 7 sp a month** — a year of discipline buys him a suit of Studded Leather and little else. **The unskilled man does not clear anything**; at 25 sp a month against a 43 sp cost, he cannot live alone at all, and doesn't. The servant's wage is the same 1 sp, but her board — food and a room, some 9 cp a day in kind — is worth nearly as much again as her pay. It is a comfortable enough arrangement, and almost none of it is coin she can save, or leave with.

A **cleric's** income depends entirely on the saint served and the office held. A Mendicant may own nothing by vow and eat by alms. A well-placed servant of the Throne or of Trade may draw 100 sp in a day. The Church is not one economy but eleven.

### Property

None of it compares to owning land. A burgher of Waldheim with a rowhome — his shop on the ground floor, his family on the second, and four renters on each of the two floors above — collects **40 cp a day** in rents, some **1,460 sp a year**, before his shop earns a penny. He lives above his own trade and pays no rent to anyone.

A property is worth roughly **two to five times its annual rents** — call that rowhome **3,000 to 7,300 sp**. Against a **property tax of 10% of its value**, half that rent roll goes straight back out again. What remains is a decent living and a real risk: the tax is owed on the building's worth whether or not a single room is let, and the towns of Avilund are still refilling after a long decline. There are more rooms than renters in most places. Roofs fail. Buildings burn.

This is why property in Avilund is cheap to buy and hard to hold, and why a moderately successful man is so often a nervous one.

### What Things Cost

For orientation, in a working man's terms — he earns 2 sp a day and clears 7 sp a month:

| Thing | Cost | In his terms |
|---|---|---|
| A night's bed | 5 cp | a quarter of a day |
| A day's food and drink | 5 cp | a quarter of a day |
| Leather armour | 10 sp | five days' wages |
| A Healer's Kit | 10 sp | five days' wages |
| Studded Leather | 25 sp | two weeks |
| Chain Mail | 150 sp | three months' wages — two years of saving |
| Full Plate | 1,500 sp | two and a half years' wages — a third of a rowhome |

**Full Plate is not equipment. It is property.** A harness of plate costs what a building costs, and it is owned by the sort of people who own buildings. Adventurers do not save up for it; they are granted it, left it, or take it off someone.
