---
title: "Book Four — Of the Sancti Minores"
order: 5
---

Beyond the three-and-thirty of the higher orders stretch the **Sancti Minores**, the lesser saints — countless, and of every origin. Some did one holy deed in an obscure corner of the world and were never heard of again; some keep a single chapel in a single valley; the most have no living worshipper at all, and survive only as a name in a parish register or a relic in a forgotten reliquary.

Among them, and not the least, is **Caanan**, whom the older accounts (this author's own first edition among them) once numbered with the early saints. Yet the truth, which honesty compels, is that Caanan has no canon, no order, and no known follower; his name is invoked only by the common man when misfortune strikes, and the schoolmen are divided whether he be a saint at all or a Hellion in saint's clothing. He is rightly the patron of the very thing he has become: the Forgotten. Let him stand, then, first among the Minores — a caution to every saint that even a place among the early Pantheon is no proof against the long erosion of the years.

*Here ends the Account.*
