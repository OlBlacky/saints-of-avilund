# Armour & Equipment

## How Armour Works

Armour provides an **Equipment Bonus** to physical Defence Targets — **Str, Dex and Con** only. Mental Defence Targets (Int, Wis, Cha) are protected by other equipment such as cloaks, rings and amulets (to be defined).

Different characters achieve similar Defence Targets through different means:
- A **Rogue** invests AP heavily in Dex score and Dex Defence Bonus; light armour tops it up
- A **Fighter** invests less in stats and relies on equipment doing the heavy lifting
- A **Fighter in Plate + Heavy Shield** combines both — solid stats and maximum equipment bonus

This means the attacker always has options — a heavily armoured Fighter can still be attacked through Int, Wis or Cha, bypassing physical armour entirely.

## Armour Proficiencies

Every **Class** grants some Armour Proficiencies; some **Paths** grant additional ones. You receive **all** of your Class's and Path's armour proficiencies automatically.

*Example — the **Soldier** comes with Light Armour, Medium Armour, and Light Shield. The **Vanguard** Path additionally grants Heavy Armour and Heavy Shield.*

The five proficiencies and what they unlock:

| Proficiency | Unlocks |
|---|---|
| Light Armour | Padded, Leather |
| Medium Armour | Breastplate, Scale Mail |
| Heavy Armour | Chain Mail, Plate Mail |
| Light Shield | Light Shield |
| Heavy Shield | Heavy Shield |

Beyond your Class/Path grants, **any character may buy any Armour Proficiency** for a **Minor Advance** — but a proficiency gained this way stays at base and **can never be advanced**. Only your Class/Path armour proficiencies climb the scale below.

### Non-Proficiency Penalty

Using armour or a shield you aren't proficient with imposes a penalty to **attack rolls and physical skill checks**:

| Without proficiency in… | Penalty |
|---|---|
| Light Armour · Medium Armour · Light Shield | −1 |
| Heavy Armour · Heavy Shield | −2 |

Being proficient is +0.

### Advancing a Proficiency

Like Weapon Proficiencies, each Armour Proficiency has a scale improved through **Minor Advances**, taken in order; the final **+1 AC** step in every track unlocks at **Level 5**.

- **DR** = Damage Reduction, subtracted from incoming physical damage. Armour grants no DR by default — DR is gained only through these advances.
- **AC** = the Defence Target the armour contributes to.

**Light Armour**
1. DR 1
2. *(Level 5)* +1 AC

**Medium Armour**
1. No Speed penalty (removes the −5')
2. +1 DR
3. *(Level 5)* +1 AC

**Heavy Armour**
1. Speed penalty reduced to −5' (from −10')
2. No Speed penalty
3. +1 DR
4. *(Level 5)* +1 AC

**Light Shield**
1. +1 Shield DR
2. *(Level 5)* +1 AC

**Heavy Shield**
1. No Speed penalty (removes the −5')
2. +1 Shield DR
3. *(Level 5)* +1 AC

### Armour Specialisation Feats

Some Classes and Paths have access to **Armour Specialisation Feats**, gated by Class/Path. These **modify your defensive Abilities** when wearing the relevant armour type — the armour counterpart to the Weapon Specialisation Feats that modify offensive abilities (see [weapons.md](weapons.md)).

Armour Specialisation Feats can only be taken at **Level 2 or higher**.

## Balance Reference

| Build | Defence Target (example) |
|---|---|
| Rogue in Leather (Dex +3, Dex Defence Bonus +2) | 10 + 3 + 2 + 2 = **17** |
| Fighter in Chain Mail + Light Shield (Str +1, Str Defence Bonus +1) | 10 + 1 + 1 + 4 + 1 = **17** |
| Fighter in Plate Mail + Heavy Shield (Str +2, Str Defence Bonus +2) | 10 + 2 + 2 + 5 + 2 = **21** |

The Rogue and Fighter arrive at similar Defence Targets through very different AP investment paths. The Plate + Heavy Shield Fighter is the hardest to hit in the game.
