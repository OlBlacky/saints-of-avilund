# Friar

*Portfolio of Mercy*

The Friar is the body-mender of Avilund — physician of the faithful, who walks toward suffering when others turn away. In this world **Mercy is the healing of the body**: medicine, herb-craft, the tending of wounds. War belongs to the Soldier and the dead to the Occultist, so the Friar is no armoured war-priest — his gift is to keep a company whole. His three Paths run along a single spectrum of how much steel a man of Mercy will touch: the **Chaplain** who marches with the war-band, the **Confessor** who mends the soul and hunts the Black Faith, and the **Mendicant** who lifts no weapon at all and gives himself for others.

## Class Features

A Friar brings **Wisdom** as their Class Attribute and the **Service** Ability Category, with a modest **Class HP of 2**. They are trained in the **Heal** skill and in **Religion (Saintly Faith)**, and they carry only the old clerical arms — the **Hammers/Maces** and **Staves** that shed no blood. They wear no trained armour; a Friar's protection comes from his Path and his faith.

The **Service** Category has no attacks of any kind. It is quiet, deliberately underpowered healing — a base mending of little more than your Wisdom — together with blessings, wards against affliction, and the camp and social rites that hold a company together. Conspicuous, miraculous healing is the work of a seasoned saint, not a starting trick.

Your chosen **Path** adds a second Class Attribute, a second Ability Category, two more Class Skills, and further proficiencies.

## Paths

### Chaplain
The most warlike of the three — the fighting priest who marches with the war-band and takes up arms to hold the line. He heals with Wisdom and hits with Strength, but he is no Soldier: just enough armour to stand in the press, and the mace and staff he already carries.

### Confessor
The tender of souls — exorcist and inquisitor, who breaks possession, cows the wicked, and stands against the Black Faith. A debuffer: he does not out-damage a foe, he *unmakes* them, fighting with Charisma against the enemy's own resolve. His battles are partly spiritual, sometimes violent.

The least warlike — barefoot, weaponless, owning nothing, who will not raise a hand even to defend himself, only to shield others. He absorbs harm and returns it as grace: the company's Temp HP flows from his own suffering.

## Abilities

Every ability is a chassis improved over a career. Ladders show a base Rank, two Ranks bought with **Minor** Advances (m), and a top Rank bought with a **Major** (M). Where a Major Rank is gated to a level, it is noted (e.g. *L5*).

Two resolution conventions apply throughout:

- **One roll resolves the whole ability.** An ability with an attack makes a single roll; on a success, *all* of its purchased ladder effects apply at once.
- **The Ritual Option** is a reusable Minor advance offered on some abilities: instead of acting in the heat of battle, the Friar performs the ability as a rite — slower, out of combat, with **material components (a prayer book, a holy symbol, and 10 sp of incense)** — and rolls a relevant **skill at +2** against the target's DC in place of the attack.

### Forbearance — *Mendicant*

The pacifist martyr's discipline: binding **Vows**, Temp HP wrung from his own suffering, and the endurance to keep standing. No attacks.

**The Vows.** Each Vow is a passive, always-on commitment with a single advance: base **+1**, rising to **+2 at Level 5** (a Major). A Vow **cannot be broken willingly**; if a compulsion effect *forces* a breach, its benefit is lost until the character **Atones** (DM-adjudicated).

**Vow of Mercy** *(Vow)* — *The Vow:* never willingly bear arms, make an attack roll, harm an ally, or allow an ally to come to harm if you can prevent it. *Effect:* any ability you use that heals or grants Temp HP to allies is **+1** *(→ +2, L5)*.

**Vow of Poverty** *(Vow)* — *The Vow:* accumulate no personal wealth, nothing beyond the clothes on your back and the instruments of healing and your Saintly office. *Effect:* **+1** to all Armoured and Unarmoured Defences *(→ +2, L5)*.

**Vow of Abstinence** *(Vow)* — *The Vow:* never imbibe alcohol, tobacco, or similar substances, nor drink potions or willingly receive any healing or magical benefit not from a Saintly source. *Effect:* **+1** to all saves *(→ +2, L5)*.

## Reference — for the Game Master's section

Two tracks the Friar's social abilities lean on (Preach to the Saintly; the Confessor's vow and interrogations):

**NPC Attitude** — Hostile → Unfriendly → Indifferent → Friendly → Helpful.

**Hireling Morale** — Broken (flees, surrenders, or quits) → Wavering (reluctant; balks at danger) → Steady (does the job) → Resolute (stands firm, faces real danger) → Devoted (loyal unto death; will not break).
