# Character Creation & Leveling Up

## What Makes a Character

In Saints of Avilund, your character is defined by six attributes, a class (or two, or three), a set of abilities, and a record of choices made with a universal resource called **Advancement Points (AP)**.

There are no random rolls in character creation. Every choice is deliberate and paid for with AP.

---

## Advancement Points

Every character earns **100 AP per level**, from Level 1 to Level 10, for a lifetime total of 1,000 AP.

At Level 1 you begin with 100 AP. You spend it to build your character — raising attributes, learning abilities, training skills, gaining class features. When you gain a level, you receive another 100 AP to spend however you choose.

AP is the same currency at every stage of play. There are no separate systems for character creation and leveling. Everything is one coherent economy.

---

## Step by Step: Building Your Character

### Step 1 — Choose Your Class

Your first class is free. Choosing it immediately gives you:

- **+1 to one Class Attribute** *(your choice from the two listed)*
- **+1 to one Class Attack Bonus** *(for one of your class attributes)*
- **+1 to one Class Save** *(for one of your class attributes)*
- **+1 to one Class Skill** *(your choice from the four listed)*
- **2 Class Standard abilities** *(chosen from your class list)*
- **1 Class Special ability** *(chosen from your class list)*
- **1 Class Feature** *(chosen from your class list)*
- **Special Pool 1** *(for using Class Special abilities)*
- **1 free HP Advance** *(amount depends on your class's HP tier)*

Record all of these before you spend any AP.

| Class | Class Attributes | Categories | HP Tier |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Fighter** | Str + Con | Martial, Survival | Tanky |
| **Warlord** | Str + Int | Martial, Academics | Tanky |
| **Monk** | Str + Wis | Martial, Spiritual | Average |
| **Paladin** | Str + Cha | Martial, Divine | Tanky |
| **Barbarian** | Dex + Con | Primal, Survival | Tanky |
| **Rogue** | Dex + Int | Stealth, Academics | Average |
| **Ranger** | Dex + Wis | Martial, Nature | Average |
| **Bard** | Dex + Cha | Social, Academics | Average |
| **Artificer** | Con + Int | Academics, Craft | Average |
| **Druid** | Con + Wis | Nature, Primal | Average |
| **Warlock** | Con + Cha | Occult, Social | Average |
| **Wizard** | Int + Wis | Arcana, Academics | Caster |
| **Sorcerer** | Int + Cha | Arcana, Social | Caster |
| **Cleric** | Wis + Cha | Divine, Social | Average |

A class's two **Class Attributes** are your primary attributes — they are cheaper to raise, and their Attack Bonuses and Saves cost less to purchase. A class's two **Categories** define the pool of abilities available to you at reduced cost.

---

### Step 2 — Record Your Starting Numbers

Before spending any AP, fill in your character sheet with the following defaults.

**Attributes** — all six begin at 0, except the one free +1 from your class.

**Attack Bonuses and Saves** — all begin at 0, except the one free +1 each from your class.

**Hit Points** — starting HP is **10 + your Con modifier + your free HP Advance**.

| HP Tier | Free HP Advance | Classes |
|---|---|---|
| Tanky | +5 HP | Fighter, Warlord, Paladin, Barbarian |
| Average | +3 HP | Monk, Ranger, Bard, Rogue, Artificer, Druid, Warlock, Cleric |
| Caster | +2 HP | Wizard, Sorcerer |

**Speed** — your base Speed is 30'. Some armour reduces this (see [Armour & Equipment](../mechanics/armour.md)).

**Defence Targets** — each attribute's Defence Target is **10 + Score + Save + Equipment Bonus**. At this stage, before AP is spent or gear is equipped, all six sit at 10.

**Initiative** — your Initiative modifier is your Dex Score plus any relevant Class Feature bonuses. At the start of combat, roll **d20 + Initiative modifier**.

---

### Step 3 — Spend Your Advancement Points

You have 100 AP. Spend them in any order across the following categories.

---

#### Attributes

Attributes run from −5 to +5. Class Attributes are cheaper to raise.

| Rank | In-Class *(incremental)* | In-Class *(cumulative)* | Out-of-Class *(incremental)* | Out-of-Class *(cumulative)* |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| +1 | 5 | 5 | 10 | 10 |
| +2 | 10 | 15 | 20 | 30 |
| +3 | 20 | 35 | 40 | 70 |
| +4 | 30 | 65 | 60 | 130 |
| +5 | 35 | 100 | 70 | 200 |

No attribute may exceed +5.

---

#### Attack Bonuses & Saves

Each attribute has its own Attack Bonus and Save, purchased independently from the attribute score itself. Out-of-class Attack Bonuses and Saves are capped at +2.

| Rank | In-Class *(incremental)* | In-Class *(cumulative)* | Out-of-Class *(incremental)* | Out-of-Class *(cumulative)* |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| +1 | 4 | 4 | 6 | 6 |
| +2 | 8 | 12 | 12 | 18 *(max)* |
| +3 | 12 | 24 | — | — |
| +4 | 20 | 44 | — | — |
| +5 | 28 | 72 *(max)* | — | — |

---

#### Hit Points

Additional HP Advances beyond your free class advance cost 10 AP each.

| HP Tier | Cost | HP Gained |
|---|---|---|
| Tanky | 10 AP | +5 HP |
| Average | 10 AP | +3 HP |
| Caster | 10 AP | +2 HP |

---

#### Abilities

Your class bundle includes 2 Class Standard abilities and 1 Class Special. You may buy more.

| Purchase | In-Class Cost |
|---|---|
| 3rd Class Standard | 14 AP |
| 4th Class Standard | 18 AP |
| 2nd Class Special | 12 AP |
| 3rd Class Special | 22 AP |
| 1st Class Main | 10 AP |
| 2nd Class Main | 15 AP |

*Out-of-class ability costs are approximately 1.5–2× the in-class cost.*

You can also improve abilities you already own. Each ability has 5 tiers; you begin at Tier 1.

| Tier | Standard | Special | Main |
|---|---|---|---|
| → 2 | 5 AP | 4 AP | 2 AP |
| → 3 | 8 AP | 6 AP | 2 AP |
| → 4 | 12 AP | 8 AP | 3 AP |
| → 5 | 15 AP | 10 AP | 4 AP |
| **Total to max** | **40 AP** | **28 AP** | **11 AP** |

Your Special and Main Pools can be expanded with AP as well.

| Special Pool | Cost | Main Pool | Cost |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 *(free with class)* | — | 1 *(free with 1st Main ability)* | — |
| 2 | 12 AP | 2 | 10 AP |
| 3 | 22 AP | 3 | 15 AP |

---

#### Skills

Each class has 4 Class Skills. In-class skills cost 1 AP per rank and are capped at +5. Out-of-class skills are capped at +2 and cost more.

| | In-Class | Out-of-Class |
|---|---|---|
| Cap | +5 | +2 |
| Cost per +1 | 1 AP | +1: 4 AP / +2: additional 8 AP |
| Free from class | +1 to 1 Class Skill | — |

For the full list of skills and which attributes apply to each, see [Skills](skills.md).

---

#### Class Features

Your class bundle includes 1 free Feature. Additional features cost AP.

| Feature Type | In-Class Cost |
|---|---|
| Standard Feature | 8 AP |
| Pinnacle Defence Feature | 20 AP *(requires +2 Save in the relevant attribute)* |

For the full list of available features, see [Classes](classes.md).

---

#### Additional Classes

A second class costs **50 AP**. A third costs **100 AP**. A fourth is not permitted.

Taking a second class delivers the same starting bundle as your first — free bonuses, 2 Standard abilities, 1 Special, 1 Feature, and a new independent Special Pool. Once you own a class, all of its advances are permanently available at in-class cost.

## Leveling Up

Gaining a level is straightforward: **receive 100 AP and spend it however you choose.**

There are no mandatory choices tied to any given level. No prescribed ability score increases, no fixed feature slots. Your character grows in whatever direction you invest in.

The only constraint is what you can afford. Raising an attribute to +3 is accessible early; pushing it to +5 requires sustained investment over multiple levels.

---

## Multi-Classing

You may own up to three classes. Each grants its full starting bundle along with a new, independent Special Pool.

Once you own a class, its abilities and features are always available at in-class cost — you never need to declare which class you are "leveling in." A character with two classes has two Special Pools refreshing independently on a Short Rest.

Taking a second or third class is a significant AP investment. Weigh the breadth it gives you against the depth you are deferring.

---

## A Note on Power and Level

A Level 10 character is not ten times as powerful as a Level 1 character. The AP economy is designed so that growth at every level is meaningful, but the gap between an experienced character and a newcomer is one of **specialization and breadth** — not raw power. A Level 2 character is not twice as capable as a Level 1 character.

This matters at the table. Characters of different levels can adventure together without lower-level characters feeling useless or higher-level characters feeling invincible.
