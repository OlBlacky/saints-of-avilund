# Weapons

## Weapon Proficiencies

Every weapon belongs to one of **17 Weapon Proficiency groups**. Being proficient in a group means you use any weapon in it without penalty.

| Melee | Ranged & Thrown |
|---|---|
| Axes | Bows |
| Heavy Blades | Crossbows |
| Light Blades | Slings |
| Hammers/Maces | Thrown |
| Picks | Pistols |
| Flails/Chains | Rifles |
| Polearms | Grenades |
| Spears/Lances | |
| Unarmed/Natural | |
| Staves | |

*(Adapted from the D&D 3.5 weapon-group scheme: merged Bludgeons + the Hammers half of Picks & Hammers into Hammers/Maces; split Picks out on their own; folded Monk/Natural/Unarmed into Unarmed/Natural; dropped Double Weapons and Tribal; replaced Firearms with separate Pistols and Rifles; added Grenades and Staves.)*

---

## Advancement: Gaining Proficiencies

- Every **Class** comes with **1–3 Weapon Proficiencies**, and you receive **all** of them when you take the Class — no choosing.
- Every **Path** likewise comes with **1–3 Weapon Proficiencies**, and you receive **all** of them.
- The number (1–3) depends on the martial nature of the Class or Path.
- Example — the **Soldier** comes with **Heavy Blades, Polearms, and Crossbows**.

Beyond your Class/Path grants, **any character may buy any Weapon Proficiency** for a **Minor Advance** — but a proficiency gained this way sits at +0 forever and **can never be advanced** (see below).

### Proficiency Bonus

| Situation | Attack modifier |
|---|---|
| **Proficient** in the weapon's group | +0 |
| **Not proficient** | −1 |

### Improving a Proficiency

- Only your **Class** and **Path** proficiencies can be advanced. Proficiencies bought separately with a Minor Advance stay at +0.
- A **Minor Advance** can buy **+1** to a Class/Path proficiency.
- At **5th level**, you may buy a **second +1** in that proficiency.
- A proficiency is **capped at +2**.

### Weapon Specialization Feats

Some Classes have access to **Weapon Specialization Feats**. Beyond any raw bonus, these **unlock the Effects of selected abilities** when wielding the relevant weapon group — tying a character's weapon choice into their ability roster rather than just their to-hit.

Weapon Specialization Feats can only be taken at **Level 2 or higher**.

## Weapon Traits

Every weapon has the following traits:

| Trait | Description |
|---|---|
| **Weapon Group** | One of the 17 Weapon Proficiency groups above; determines proficiency and specialization (e.g. Heavy Blades, Hammers/Maces) |
| **Type** | Piercing, Slashing, or Blunt |
| **Damage** | Die rolled on a hit |
| **Hands** | One-handed (1H) or Two-handed (2H) |
| **Weight** | In lbs |
| **Cost** | In-world price *(costs to be revised once currency is defined)* |
| **Range** | *(Ranged and Thrown weapons only)* Three increments in feet |
| **Special Properties** | See below |

### Range Penalties
| Increment | Penalty |
|---|---|
| 1st | No penalty |
| 2nd | −2 to attack |
| 3rd | −4 to attack |
| Beyond 3rd | Out of range |

## Special Properties

| Property | Effect |
|---|---|
| **Finesse** | May use Dex instead of Str for attack and damage rolls |
| **Reach** | Melee range is 10' instead of 5' |
| **Thrown** | May be used as a ranged weapon; has range increments |
| **Versatile** | May be used one- or two-handed; two-handed increases the damage die by one step |
| **Loading** | Must spend a Minor Action to reload between shots |
| **Concealable** | May be hidden on the body |
| **Defensive** | Grants +1 to a Defence Target while wielded |
